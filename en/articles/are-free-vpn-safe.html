Are Free VPNs Safe?

January 5, 2025 16 min read VPN GID Experts

Popular Myth About 'Free Cheese'

"If the product is free, then you are the product." This phrase perfectly describes the reality of free VPN services. In 2025, the market is flooded with hundreds of applications promising free protection and anonymity. But are they really safe? According to cybersecurity research, 84% of free VPN apps contain serious vulnerabilities or intentionally collect user data.

Main Risks of Free VPNs

Data Collection and Sale

Many free VPNs make money by selling user data to advertisers. Your browsing history, location and online habits become a commodity.

Weak Encryption

To save resources, free VPNs often use outdated encryption protocols or transmit data in plain text, making you vulnerable.

Ads and Tracking

Aggressive advertising, pop-ups and built-in trackers not only interfere with work, but also collect additional information about your preferences.

Limitations and Low Speed

Free versions have traffic limits, few servers and low speeds. This makes them practically useless for everyday use.

Malware

Research shows that 38% of free VPN apps contain malicious code that can steal data or infect your device.

No Support

Most free VPNs don't provide technical support. When problems arise, you're left alone with difficulties.

How to Check VPN Reliability

Before choosing a VPN service, conduct a simple check:

  1. Check real IP address on sites like whatismyipaddress.com
  2. Make sure there are no DNS leaks using dnsleaktest.com
  3. Check connection speed before and after VPN connection
  4. Study company's privacy policy
  5. Look for independent reviews and security audits

Free VPN Business Models: How They Make Money

Understanding how free VPNs make money will help you better assess the risks:

1. Selling User Data

The most common monetization method. Free VPNs collect information about your site visits, search queries, purchases and sell this data to advertisers, data brokers and analytics companies. Your traffic becomes a commodity.

2. Ad Injection

VPN can inject ads directly into web pages you browse. They earn revenue from every impression or click. Pop-ups with malicious links are particularly dangerous.

3. Cryptocurrency Mining

Some VPNs use users' CPU and internet connection for cryptocurrency mining. This slows down the device, increases power consumption and can cause overheating.

4. Paid Subscriptions (Freemium Model)

Honest way of earning: offering limited free version with ability to upgrade to paid plan. However, such providers are in minority.

Real Cases of Data Compromise

History knows many scandals with free VPN services:

Hola VPN Case

In 2015, it was revealed that Hola VPN was selling its users' traffic as part of the Luminati botnet. Users' computers were used for DDoS attacks and other illegal activities. More than 47 million users were compromised.

SuperVPN Data Leak

In 2021, researchers discovered that SuperVPN left unprotected database with logs of 21 million users, including email addresses, IP addresses, location data and visited sites.

Technical Vulnerabilities of Free VPNs

Weak or No Encryption

  • Using outdated protocols like PPTP
  • Weak encryption keys (less than 128 bits)
  • Lack of Perfect Forward Secrecy
  • Vulnerable SSL/TLS certificates

Privacy Issues

  • DNS and WebRTC leaks
  • Lack of Kill Switch function
  • Logging all user activities
  • Telemetry data collection

Legal Risks and Jurisdiction Issues

Many free VPNs are based in countries with unclear data protection legislation:

  • China - state censorship and internet control
  • USA - requirements to transfer data to intelligence agencies
  • Russia - laws on personal data storage
  • Offshore zones - lack of proper regulation

Hidden Costs of "Free" VPN

Using free VPN can cost more than paid one:

  • Identity theft and financial fraud
  • Malware infection and system recovery
  • Increased traffic consumption due to ads
  • Reduced device performance
  • Time spent solving security problems

Frequently Asked Questions About Free VPNs

Can I use free VPN for streaming?

Highly not recommended. Free VPNs have speed and traffic limitations, making video viewing uncomfortable. Additionally, most streaming platforms actively block free VPNs.

Are there completely safe free VPNs?

Few providers offer honest freemium models: ProtonVPN, Windscribe, TunnelBear. But even they have significant limitations on speed, servers and traffic.

Can I trust VPN from Google Play or App Store?

No guarantees. Many malicious VPNs successfully pass moderation in official app stores. Always study reviews, privacy policy and developer reputation.
