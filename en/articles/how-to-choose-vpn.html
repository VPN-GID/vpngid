What I Check When Choosing VPN? 7 Key Criteria

I check every VPN against this checklist before recommending it in the ranking. If any point fails — the service doesn't make it to the top.

1. Security and Encryption

The main function of VPN is to protect your data. Quality service should use modern encryption protocols and have proven reputation in security.

What to look for:

  • AES-256 encryption
  • WireGuard, OpenVPN, IKEv2 protocols
  • Kill Switch feature
  • DNS leak protection
  • Independent security audits

2. Connection Speed

VPN inevitably slows down internet connection due to encryption process. However, quality services minimize this slowdown to 10-20%.

Factors affecting speed:

  • Number and quality of servers
  • Geographic proximity to servers
  • Used protocol (WireGuard is the fastest)
  • Server load

3. Privacy Policy

No-logs policy means provider doesn't keep logs of your internet activities. This is critically important for ensuring anonymity.

What should be in the policy:

  • Clear no-logs policy statement
  • Company jurisdiction information
  • Details about what data is collected
  • Independent privacy policy audits

4. Server Network

Large number of servers in different countries ensures better speed, availability and ability to bypass geographic restrictions.

Optimal characteristics:

  • Minimum 1000+ servers
  • 50+ countries
  • Servers in regions you need
  • Own servers (not rental)

5. Cost and Plans

Don't overpay for brand. AdGuard with promo code costs $2.5/mo and works like $10 services. Check prices, money-back guarantee, and coupon availability. I collect current discounts on the coupons page.

What to check:

  • Money-back guarantee minimum 30 days — test risk-free
  • Annual plan discounts — 2-3x cheaper
  • No hidden fees or auto-renewals without warning
  • Free trial or cheap first month

6. Customer Support

Quality technical support will help quickly solve problems and get help setting up VPN on various devices.

What's important:

  • 24/7 support
  • Live chat on website
  • Knowledge base and FAQ
  • Support in your language

Warning: Red Flags

Avoid VPN providers that: offer completely free services without restrictions, are based in countries with strict data laws (USA, UK, Australia), have history of data breaches, don't provide clear privacy policy information.

What to Look for When Reading Reviews

User reviews can be helpful, but it's important to distinguish honest reviews from promotional materials or fake reviews.

  • Look for detailed reviews with specific usage examples
  • Pay attention to mentions of both pros and cons
  • Check reviews on independent platforms
  • Be wary of overly enthusiastic reviews
  • Look for reviews from technical experts and specialized sites

Detailed VPN Protocol Comparison

Choosing the right protocol is critically important for security and performance. Each protocol has its advantages and disadvantages:

WireGuard - Modern Standard

  • Currently the fastest protocol
  • Minimal battery consumption on mobile devices
  • Modern cryptography (ChaCha20, Curve25519)
  • Fast reconnection when switching networks

OpenVPN - Proven Reliability

  • Open source, passed multiple audits
  • Excellent compatibility with various devices
  • Flexible security settings
  • Can work through TCP or UDP

IKEv2/IPSec - Mobile Optimization

  • Fast reconnection when connection is lost
  • Good performance on mobile devices
  • Built-in support in Windows and iOS
  • Resilient to IP address changes

Dangers of Free VPNs

Many free VPNs make money by selling user data, injecting ads, or cryptocurrency mining. Some even contain malware. Remember: if the product is free, you are the product.

How to Properly Test VPN Before Purchase

Most quality VPNs offer trial periods or money-back guarantees. Use this time for thorough testing:

Speed Testing

  1. Measure internet speed without VPN
  2. Connect to nearest server and repeat test
  3. Check speed on servers in different countries
  4. Test at different times of day

Leak Testing

  • Use sites to check for IP leaks
  • Check for DNS leaks
  • Test for WebRTC leaks
  • Ensure Kill Switch works

VPN for Special Needs

For Streaming

  • Large number of servers in USA and Europe
  • High speed for 4K video
  • Special servers for Netflix, BBC iPlayer
  • SmartDNS for devices that don't support VPN

For Torrenting

  • Strict no-logs policy
  • Optimized P2P servers
  • Reliable Kill Switch
  • Port forwarding support

For Gaming

  • Minimal latency (ping)
  • Stable connection without drops
  • Servers near game servers
  • DDoS protection

Common VPN Selection Mistakes

Choosing Only by Price

The cheapest VPN is not always the best choice. Savings can result in security and performance issues.

Ignoring Jurisdiction

VPN provider's registration country is critical for data protection. Avoid companies from 5/9/14-Eyes countries.

Carelessness About Logging Policy

Many users don't read privacy policies. VPN that keeps logs can negate all protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many servers should a good VPN have?

Minimum 1000 servers in 50+ countries. More servers means less load and better performance.

Can one VPN be used on multiple devices?

Most VPNs allow using one subscription on 5-10 devices simultaneously. Check limits with your provider.

Does VPN affect device battery life?

Yes, but modern protocols like WireGuard minimize power consumption. Battery impact is usually 5-15%.
